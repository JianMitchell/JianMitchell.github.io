Understanding SDLC Methodologies

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) represents a structured systematic way of building software systems. Therefore, various methodologies were built on the premise of addressing different needs based on a projects structure, team dynamics, and business requirements to implement software systems efficiently and robustly.

This website will explore some of the fundamental SDLC methodologies that are currently prevalent today in Computer Science. Each approach offers different ways of handling software systems, while having advantages and disadvantages based on time, efficiency and the approach in the development of software.

Why SDLC Matters

SDLC methodologies are the core frameworks that determine a projects implementation. Basically, it effectively determines whether something gets delivered faster or slower based on a pre-determined approach on handling software systems. These established processes determine the requirements for gathering, designing, implementing, testing, and maintaining a projects flow to ensure company expectations.
